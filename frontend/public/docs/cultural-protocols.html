Cultural Protocols

Guidelines for ethical handling of Indigenous knowledge and cultural heritage in research data

Introduction to Cultural Protocols

Commitment to Ethical Research

DOCiD recognizes the importance of respecting Indigenous knowledge systems, traditional cultural expressions, and community protocols when managing research data. These guidelines ensure that research involving Indigenous and traditional knowledge is conducted ethically and with appropriate community consent.

Cultural Sensitivity Framework
Essential All research contexts

DOCiD implements cultural protocols based on international standards and African indigenous knowledge frameworks:

  • Free, Prior, and Informed Consent (FPIC): Community consent before research begins
  • Benefit Sharing: Ensuring communities benefit from research outcomes
  • Cultural Attribution: Proper acknowledgment of traditional knowledge sources
  • Access Controls: Respecting community decisions about data sharing
  • Return of Knowledge: Providing research results back to communities

Ethical Research Guidelines

ü§ù Before Research

  • Establish contact with community leaders
  • Obtain Free, Prior, Informed Consent
  • Develop benefit-sharing agreements
  • Identify cultural protocols
  • Assign cultural advisors

üìù During Research

  • Follow community protocols
  • Regular community consultation
  • Respect access restrictions
  • Apply appropriate TK labels
  • Document consent processes

üì§ After Research

  • Return results to community
  • Honor benefit-sharing agreements
  • Maintain access restrictions
  • Provide ongoing attribution
  • Support community capacity building

üõ°Ô∏è Ongoing Responsibilities

  • Monitor data usage
  • Enforce access restrictions
  • Update community on research impact
  • Facilitate community review
  • Respect evolving protocols
